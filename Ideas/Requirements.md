**National Deep Tech Challenge 2026: Project Requirements**

Welcome to the official requirements guide for the **National Deep Tech Challenge 2026**. This program is designed to move beyond traditional \"weekend prototypes\" and focus on turning real-world science into scalable businesses.

**📅 Workshop Series**

To ensure all teams are prepared for the final hackathon, two mandatory workshops will be held at **Makerspace@UM** (Ground Level, Block B, Kompleks Perdana Siswa, Universiti Malaya).

**Workshop 1: Market & Problem Framing**

**Date & Time:** 12 May \| 1:00 PM -- 4:00 PM

This session focuses on training participants to identify who actually needs a specific technology and what real-world problem it solves. The goal is to prevent the common failure of deep tech failing to map to a market need.

- **Key Takeaways:**

  - A framework to evaluate market fit for deep tech IP.

  - A shortlisted IP the team is committed to.

  - A defined problem statement and target user profile.

- **Pre-Workshop Checklist:**

  - Confirm attendance in the calendar.

  - Explore the UM IP Library.

  - Shortlist 2-3 potential IPs as a team.

  - Identify the team's strongest domain (e.g., MedTech, AgriTech, GreenTech).

**Workshop 2: Commercial Model & Pitch Fundamentals**

**Date & Time:** 13 May \| 1:00 PM -- 4:00 PM

Once a market has been identified, this workshop focuses on monetization and compelling presentation. Participants will learn to structure commercial models around licensed IP for judges and investors.

- **Key Takeaways:**

  - A revenue model appropriate for deep tech commercialization.

  - A clear understanding of judge and investor expectations.

  - A rough pitch structure to be developed into the final deck.

- **Workshop Checklist:**

  - Confirm attendance in the calendar.

  - Finalize and confirm the IP selection.

  - Develop initial thoughts on target customers.

  - Outline early assumptions regarding the business model.

**🚀 Final Deliverables**

By the end of the **2-Day 1-Night Hackathon**, every team must submit two primary components:

1\. A Structured Pitch Deck

Your presentation must include, but is not limited to, the following sections:

- **The Selected IP:** Detailed identification of the chosen technology.

- **Market Opportunity:** Identifying target users and demand.

- **Commercialization Strategy:** How the IP will be brought to market.

- **Business Model:** Revenue streams and cost structures.

- **Go-To-Market Plan:** Steps for initial launch and growth.

- **Competitive Landscape:** Analysis of current market alternatives.

- **Scaling Potential:** Long-term growth prospects.

- **The Ask:** What is required from investors or partners.

2\. Product Prototype/Demo

This is not necessarily a fully coded software but a demonstration of the solution\'s logic. Components include:

- Wireframes and Mockups.

- System flow and architecture.

- The Prototype itself (physical or digital).

**Important Note:** We are not testing your coding skills; we are testing your thinking.

**⚖️ Judging Criteria**

Submissions will be evaluated based on five core pillars:

| **Criteria**               | **Description**                                                            | **Weight** |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| **Application of UM IP**   | Is the IP used meaningfully to create real value in the solution?          | **25%**    |
| **Solution Feasibility**   | Can this realistically be built? Is the approach logical and practical?    | **25%**    |
| **Problem Clarity**        | Is the problem clearly defined, real, and relevant?                        | **20%**    |
| **Market Opportunity**     | Who are the target users? Is there real demand or a viable use case?       | **20%**    |
| **Team Execution & Pitch** | Clarity of presentation, strength of storytelling, and team understanding. | **10%**    |
